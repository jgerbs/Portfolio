About Me

Hello, my name is Jack Gerber. I am a recent BCIT CST graduate, and I am eager to begin my career in the security and tech industry. Since I was a kid, I’ve always loved problem solving and understanding how things work. I originally got into development and spent a lot of time experimenting with game projects, websites, and anything that let me build something cool. As I learned more about cybersecurity and saw how real attacks impact people and businesses, my focus shifted. I knew I wanted to be someone who helps protect systems and make the internet a safer place. That mix of investigation, defense, and technical challenge is what really motivates me.

Before getting into tech, I spent several years working in the culinary industry. It was a demanding environment, but it taught me to stay organized, work under pressure, and communicate clearly with the people around me. When COVID paused my plans to attend culinary school, I used that time to explore other interests and quickly found myself back in front of a computer, writing code. From there, everything moved naturally. Through BCIT I gained strong experience with software engineering fundamentals, secure coding, Linux and operating systems, networking, authentication, relational databases like SQL Server, PostgreSQL, and SQLite, full stack development, and hands-on cybersecurity concepts. These skills, combined with my work ethic and curiosity, are what I bring into every project and team I’m part of.

A Misogi is a challenge meant to push you past your comfort zone and show you what you are actually capable of. It comes from an old Japanese purification ritual and was modernized into a simple idea: once a year, choose something so difficult that your chance of success is only about fifty percent. It is about growth, resilience, and learning what you can do when things get uncomfortable.

I started my Misogi list in 2023 as a way to push myself intentionally and learn more about how I handle difficult situations. Each challenge has taught me something different about mindset, focus, and staying composed when things get tough. These lessons shape the way I learn, build, and solve problems every day.
